We use your data only for the following purposes:

  • To operate the service: Your organization profile, uploaded documents, and grant history power the discovery engine and AI proposal drafts. Without this data, Grange AI cannot function.

  • To communicate with you: We send transactional emails (password resets, billing receipts, account notices). With your permission, we may send product updates. You can opt out of marketing emails at any time.

  • To ensure security: Log data and usage patterns help us detect fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access.
  • To comply with law: We may process your data to comply with applicable legal obligations, such as responding to lawful requests from public authorities.

We will never: sell your data to advertisers, share your grant content with other organizations, use your proposals to train AI models without your explicit consent, or use your data in ways not described in this policy.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for their own marketing purposes. We share data only in these limited circumstances:

Infrastructure & Hosting

How we build and operate the platform

Cloud infrastructure

Grange AI runs on cloud providers with SOC 2 Type II certification. Our infrastructure is isolated per environment (production, staging, development) with no shared secrets between environments.

Data isolation

Each organization's grant data, profiles, and generated content are stored with logical isolation. We do not co-mingle data across organizations. Your data cannot be accessed by or exposed to other customers.

Network security

All traffic is routed through a web application firewall (WAF) that blocks common attack patterns. Internal services communicate over private network interfaces and are not exposed to the public internet.

Uptime & resilience

We target 99.9% monthly uptime. Critical services run across multiple availability zones. Automated failover and health checks minimize downtime during incidents.

Data Protection & Encryption

How we protect your data at every layer

Encryption in transit: All communication between your browser and Grange AI is encrypted using TLS 1.3, the most current standard. We enforce HTTPS across all endpoints and reject insecure connections.
Encryption at rest: All database records and uploaded files are encrypted at rest using AES-256. Encryption keys are managed separately from the data they protect and are rotated on a regular schedule.
Password security: Passwords are hashed using bcrypt with a per-user salt. We never store passwords in plain text. Grange AI employees cannot retrieve your password. Only a reset is possible.
Backups: Your data is backed up daily with point-in-time recovery capability. Backups are encrypted and stored in a geographically separate region. Backup restoration is tested quarterly.
Payment data: We do not store credit card numbers or CVV codes. Payments are processed by Stripe, which is PCI DSS Level 1 certified. We store only a payment token and basic billing metadata.

Access Controls

Who can see your data and under what conditions

Role-based access

Internal access to customer data is restricted by role. Only a minimal number of engineers with a legitimate operational need can access production data, and only through a controlled access request process.

Multi-factor authentication

All internal access to production systems requires multi-factor authentication. We enforce phishing-resistant MFA (hardware keys or authenticator apps) for all team members with production access.

Audit logging

Every internal access to customer data is logged with a timestamp, user identity, and reason. Logs are stored separately from application data and reviewed for anomalies on a regular basis.

Principle of least privilege

Team members are granted only the minimum access required for their role. Access is reviewed and revoked when no longer needed. Contractor access is time-limited and scoped to specific tasks.

Application Security

How we build and test for security

Secure development lifecycle: Security is integrated into our development process from the start. Engineers follow secure coding guidelines, code changes are reviewed by peers before merging, and we run automated static analysis on every pull request.

Dependency management: We use automated tooling to monitor our software dependencies for known vulnerabilities and apply updates on a regular schedule. Critical security patches are applied as soon as they are available.

Vulnerability scanning: We run automated vulnerability scans against our infrastructure and application on a regular basis. Findings are triaged by severity and resolved according to a defined SLA.

OWASP Top 10: Our application is designed and tested against the OWASP Top 10 most common web application security risks, including SQL injection, cross-site scripting (XSS), cross-site request forgery (CSRF), and broken authentication.
